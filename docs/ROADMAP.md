Reports to build (test LLM generation):

Managing General Agents (MGAs) generate specialized reports to satisfy three distinct stakeholders: risk-bearing carriers, regulatory bodies, and internal executive teams. Because MGAs operate under delegated authority, their reporting focus centers on technical underwriting profitability, exposure management, and distribution health. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
The most common reports generated by MGAs are structured below by their operational domain.

---

## 📋 1. Carrier & Capacity Provider Reports (Bordereaux)

Bordereaux are the single most critical, non-negotiable reports an MGA generates. Usually produced monthly or quarterly, these granular, line-by-line datasets prove the MGA is underwriting within its mandated authority limits. [6, 7, 8, 9]

- Premium Bordereau: Detailed logs of every policy issued, renewed, or canceled. It includes policy numbers, effective dates, gross written premium (GWP), net premium, and the MGA override commission. [10, 11, 12, 13]
- Risk / Underwriting Bordereau: Architectural risk metrics for the carrier. It tracks specific exposures like property construction types, geographic coordinates, limits exposed, and deductible levels. [5, 14, 15]
- Claims Bordereau: If the MGA has claims-settling authority, this reports First Notice of Loss (FNOL) logs, open claim statuses, reserves set, and payments disbursed. [5, 16]

---

## 📈 2. Portfolio Performance & Underwriting Reports

These internal and external metrics track whether the MGA's niche programs are actually making money. [5, 17]

- Loss Ratio Reports: Tracks incurred losses against earned premiums. MGAs monitor the Paid Loss Ratio, Incurred Loss Ratio, and Ultimate Loss Ratio to secure their profit-sharing commissions. [10, 11, 18, 19]
- Triangulation / Loss Development Reports: Show how claims evolve over time. This helps carriers and reinsurers assess if the MGA is reserving accurately or if there is adverse reserve development. [10, 20]
- Rate Adequacy Reports: Monitors whether the premium prices being charged are keeping pace with inflation and shifts in risk severity. [5]
- Quote-to-Bind (Hit Ratio) Reports: Tracks operational efficiency. It highlights what percentage of submission quotes are successfully converted into bound policies. [17, 21, 22]

---

## 🗺️ 3. Risk Exposure & Catastrophe Management Reports

Carriers strictly limit how much total risk an MGA can aggregate in a single geographical area or class of business. [5, 15]

- Geographic Concentration Reports: Visualizes policy aggregation by zip code or cat-zones (e.g., hurricane or wildfire zones) to prevent catastrophic over-exposure. [14]
- Total Insured Value (TIV) Reports: Sums up the aggregate dollar limit of all active policies in a program to prove the MGA hasn't breached its overall capacity treaty limits. [5, 15]

---

## 💰 4. Fiduciary Accounting & Financial Reports

MGAs handle premium dollars on behalf of others, requiring stringent premium accounting reports. [17, 23, 24, 25]

- Premium Trust Account Reconciliation: Audits the fiduciary bank accounts where premium cash is held before being remitted to the carrier.
- Aged Accounts Receivable: Tracks past-due premium balances from sub-agents, retail brokers, or direct insureds to prevent policy cancellation friction.
- Commission Statements: Statements detailing the split between the sub-agent/broker commission and the MGA's retained override commission. [11, 12, 17, 26, 27]

---

## ⚖️ 5. Compliance & Regulatory Reports

- Surplus Lines Stamping Reports: For MGAs writing non-admitted business, these reports prove state taxes and stamping fees were properly calculated and paid.
- Quarterly Insurer Account Reports: Statutorily required in many jurisdictions (such as under NAIC guidelines or specific state statutes like Louisiana's Act 488) to formally report written/earned premiums and incurred-but-not-reported (IBNR) claims.
- Producer Licensing & Appointment Logs: Verifies that every sub-agent who submitted business to the MGA held an active license and valid carrier appointment at the exact time of binding. [2, 10, 28, 29, 30]

🏛️ Lloyd’s / London Market Requirements
The London market operates on rigid, centralized standards governed by the Lloyd's Market Association (LMA) and the Market Business Glossary. MGAs must submit data using specific V5.0 or V5.2 standardized templates, typically processed through central systems like the Lloyd's Premium and Claims Submissions Office (LPCS).Standardized Core Bordereaux: Unlike domestic carriers who accept custom spreadsheets, Lloyd’s mandates three specific, non-negotiable formats:Risk Bordereaux (V5): Captures ultra-granular data points like Lloyd’s Risk Codes, Section Numbers, and Coverholder Reference Numbers.Premium Bordereaux (V5): Records currency splits (e.g., USD, GBP, EUR) and calculates specific Lloyd’s market deductions, gross premium, and net premium.Claims Bordereaux (V5): Tracks unique Lloyd’s Claim References (UCR) and distinguishes between paid claims, outstanding reserves, and Lloyd's fees.The Crystal Report (Lloyd’s Regulatory): A highly specialized report used to ensure compliance with global tax and regulatory rules across different countries where Lloyd's syndicates operate.Syndicate Split Reports: Because Lloyd’s lineslips are often written by a "subscription" of multiple syndicates (e.g., Syndicate A takes 40%, Syndicate B takes 30%), the MGA must generate reports that perfectly fraction premium, risk, and claims liability across the entire signing panel.

🇺🇸 Traditional / Domestic Carrier Requirements
Domestic carriers (such as US or European standard lines insurers) generally prioritize local statutory compliance, actuarial monitoring, and operational pipeline metrics.Custom Format Bordereaux: Traditional carriers usually design their own proprietary Excel or CSV specifications tailored directly to their internal policy administration systems (e.g., Guidewire, Duck Creek).Statutory Accounting Reports (NAIC / Solvency II):US Market: Formats mapped directly to NAIC Annual Statement lines of business (Exhibits of Premiums and Losses by State, commonly known as Statutory Page 14 data).European Market: Formats optimized for Solvency II Pillar 3 data reporting.Pipeline & Underwriting Audits: Heavy focus on operational metrics like submission flows, hit ratios (quote-to-bind), and processing turnaround times to manage retail broker distribution health.
